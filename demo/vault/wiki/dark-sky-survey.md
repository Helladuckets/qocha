# Dark sky survey

A running measurement of how dark Harbor Hill actually is, taken with a
handheld sky-quality meter at the dome door on every clear night.

## Method

Three readings at zenith after astronomical twilight, median recorded
in the log with date, temperature, and moon phase. Readings within
three days of full moon are logged but excluded from the trend.

## Findings so far

The median is 19.1 magnitudes per square arcsecond — suburban, honest,
workable. The trend line has lost about 0.1 magnitude in two years.
The single biggest local offender is the parking-lot floodlight, whose
motion sensor trips on every raccoon; on floodlight nights the zenith
reading drops by 0.3.

## What the numbers argue for

A shielded, warm-white fixture on a timer instead of the motion sensor.
The town board asked for a one-page summary with the trend chart, which
is the next writing task. If the fixture changes, the survey becomes
the before-and-after evidence.
