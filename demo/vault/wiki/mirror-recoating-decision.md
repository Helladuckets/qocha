# Mirror recoating decision

The 14-inch primary has visible coating loss at the edge and measured
reflectivity is down to roughly 78 percent. Three options were on the
table through February.

## Options considered

### Recoat the existing mirror

Ship the primary to a coating lab. Quoted at 460 dollars plus freight
and about five weeks of downtime. Standard aluminum with silicon
monoxide overcoat.

### Buy a replacement mirror

A new primary of matching figure quoted at 2,100 dollars. No downtime
because the old mirror stays in place until the new one arrives, but it
is most of the year's equipment budget.

### Do nothing this year

Reflectivity loss is gradual; visual observers barely notice. The risk
is the coating blistering further and taking the price of a simple
recoat up with it.

## The decision

Recoat, scheduled for the June lull when school groups pause. Marisol's
argument carried it: the figure of the existing mirror is excellent, and
a recoat restores everything a replacement would buy at a fifth of the
cost. Theo rearranges the loaner fleet to cover the downtime, see
[[telescope-loan-program]].

## Follow-ups

- Book the coating slot before the spring rush.
- Photograph the mirror cell wiring before teardown.
- Ask the lab about a protected-silver quote while it is there.
