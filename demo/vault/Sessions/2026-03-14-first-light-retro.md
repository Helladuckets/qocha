# 2026-03-14 — first light retro

The reopening night after the winter closure. Forty-one visitors, three
scopes running, one lesson relearned.

## What worked

The Dobsonian line moved fast because Theo staged eyepieces in labeled
trays instead of a shared case. Saturn low in the east held the longest
line of the night. The new red-light path markers kept the stairs safe
without wrecking anyone's dark adaptation.

## What did not

The dome slit stuck at the same spot as last autumn — the track needs
the wheel bearing replaced, not more grease. The corrector plate dewed
over by ten; the heater strip was in the cabinet the whole time,
unplugged.

## Decisions carried forward

- Bearing replacement goes on the June downtime list with the recoat,
  see [[mirror-recoating-decision]].
- Dew heater lives on the scope, not in the cabinet.
- Print the loaner sign-up sheet before open nights; the corkboard
  sheet filled by 8pm, see [[telescope-loan-program]].
